# Wall o'Clocks

> A grid of analog clocks forming large digital digits

Suppose you don't know how to read analogue clocks, but you have a bunch of those. They can be arranged in this manner to be turned into a giant digital clock.

Inspired by [_Humans since 1982_'s kinetic sculptures](https://kitkemp.com/meet-the-maker/2023/03/meet-the-maker-humans-since-1982/), this clock features a grid of analogue clocks that sacrifice their individuality for the greater good. Instead of each clock selfishly telling its own time, they coordinate their hands to collectively form large digits.

It's a digital clock made of analogue clocks. The best of both worlds, some might say. Others might say it's an unnecessary amount of clocks. Those people are missing the point.
