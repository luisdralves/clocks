# Lemniscate Clock

> All 24 hours mapped onto an infinity symbol

Have you ever glanced at a clock showing "8" and felt a pang of existential confusion? Is it 8 in the morning, or 8 in the evening? This ambiguity has plagued humanity for centuries.

The Lemniscate Clock solves this by mapping all 24 hours onto an infinity symbol. The hours hand rotates clockwise through the right half first, completing a full revolution at noon, when it starts revolving anti clockwise on the left half for the afternoon hours. The other hands follow the same path but from 0 to 60.

Keep in mind that the hands change length as they trace the curve. The minutes hand may be longer than the hours hand at times. This is not a design oversight and it is in fact completely acceptable and readable. The hands thicknesses remain constant and allow you to easily identify them at a glance.

A "laser sight" was added to each hand for convenience too.
