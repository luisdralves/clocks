# Daylight Clock

Before mechanical clocks standardized time into rigid, equal intervals, ancient civilizations used *temporal hours* - where the length of an hour changed with the seasons. A summer hour was long and lazy; a winter hour, short and brisk.

The Daylight Clock revives this ancient wisdom. There are always exactly 12 hours between sunrise and sunset, and 12 hours between sunset and sunrise. Seconds stretch and compress to make this work. On a long summer day, each daylight second lingers a bit longer than usual. On a short winter day, they hurry along.

The clock determines sunrise and sunset from your approximate location using solar position calculations. The background color shifts to match the temporal sky - bright blue at noon, orange at dawn and dusk, deep navy at midnight.

Note: Your browser will ask for location permission. If denied, the clock defaults to Greenwich, England - a reasonable choice for anyone who feels spiritually aligned with the Prime Meridian.
